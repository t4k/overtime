Featured Service:
Open Access Publishing Support
In our continued website updates, we have reorganized the information we provide on our Open Access Publishing Support . In June 2013, the Caltech Faculty Board adopted an Open Access Policy to help disseminate the fruits of Caltech research and scholarship as widely as possible. As part of those efforts, the Caltech Library supports open access publishing with a number of publishers. There are three primary models currently: Transformative Agreements (no APCs—article processing charges), support through Subscribe to Open (S2O) / Direct to Open (D2O) programs, and APC discount programs.
To learn more about these services and the publishers who participate—from the American Chemical Society to Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences —please visit our Open Access Publishing Support page.

It's Thesis Season!
We're fast approaching thesis season and we have a variety of resources to help you: our Caltech Theses LibGuide , monthly You & Your Thesis workshops, and multiple ways to connect with our subject Librarians . The official deadline for defending theses is May 26, and you'll then need to deposit in CaltechTHESIS by June 3. Students should carefully read through the Thesis LibGuide as it provides information that can help avoid unnecessary errors that can slow down final approval of their thesis.
Our annual 3MT (Three-Minute Thesis) Competition will be April 25th at 5:00PM. Register now and join us to cheer on your favorite contestant!

Staff News: George Porter Named Repository Coordinator

We are pleased to announce that George Porter will move into the newly formed role of Repository Coordinator at Caltech Library. As the Engineering & Applied Science Librarian, George has worked on our repositories since they were created. However, the Repository Coordinator role formalizes and funds the full-time work required to support and maintain  CaltechAUTHORS as a campus service. Please join us in congratulating George!

Caltech Library Presents: Digital Content Management @ Coalition for Networked Information

Recently, Digital Library Development team members Stephen Davison, Tommy Keswick, and Tom Morrell presented on "A Sustainable, Lightweight Approach to Digital Content Management and Publication" to the Coalition for Networked Information. As an example, they used the Caltech Library-published The Atlas of Bacterial & Archaeal Cell Structure , an open access digital textbook that offers a tour of microbial cells guided by cutting-edge 3D electron microscopy.

Vroman's @ Caltech Library Book Selection: Quantum Steampunk: The Physics of Yesterday's Tomorrow by Nicole Yunger Halpern

Winner of the PROSE Award for Best Book in Popular Science and Popular Mathematics by the Association of American Publishers

The Industrial Revolution meets the quantum-technology revolution. A steampunk adventure guide to how mind-blowing quantum physics is transforming our understanding of information and energy. In her exciting new book, intrepid Harvard-trained physicist Dr. Nicole Yunger Halpern introduces these concepts to the uninitiated with what she calls "quantum steampunk," after the fantastical genre that pairs futuristic technologies with Victorian sensibilities.
