ACS Open Access - Read & Publish Agreement

If you have recently had an article accepted for publication at a journal published by the American Chemical Society (ACS), you will see a new option for publishing your article Open Access (OA). This is because Caltech has joined in an agreement with the ACS along with other universities in the state of California that collectively leverages OA fees to make more articles open access. This may be helpful to you if your work is funded by a granting agency that requires publications to be open access (e.g. NIH, NSF, the Gates Foundation, etc.). However, you are not required to publish your work OA if you do not wish to do so, nor are you required to pay the OA fee when publishing your work if you do wish to publish Open Access.

More information can be found on this Author Info and APC Payment page from SCELC. If you have any questions regarding this agreement, please contact me.

CaltechAUTHORS Migration Complete
Our CaltechAUTHORS repository—home to over 100,000 publications written by Caltech researchers—has migrated to a new platform, InvenioRDM. Most items in CaltechAUTHORS are articles, but there are also books, book chapters, conference papers, and more. The repository is updated continuously as departments and library staff add available and recently published documents.

The Caltech Library is one of the first libraries worldwide to go live on this newest platform. InvenioRDM is the result of an international collaboration to develop a state-of-the-art institutional/data repository platform. Caltech Library has been a development partner with the CERN-based effort since its inception in 2019.

The migration to InvenioRDM enhances many available features, such as: improved landing pages with clearer metadata display and file previews; a full search engine, including searching all attached documents; a modern repository platform with automation potential; enhanced metadata capabilities; and accessibility improvements. Please note that links to publication lists have changed with the migration and we are working to update them as quickly as possible. If you need immediate assistance with updating your embedded publication list, please contact us.
Visit CaltechAUTHORS to learn more about the work being done at the Institute and the Caltech Library.

Fall Course Reserves

Course reserve requests and online bookstore additions are now open for the Fall 2023 term. The Library provides access to digital course reserves including ebooks, select chapters, and audiovisual materials. Faculty can log in to Canvas using access.caltech credentials at caltech.instructure.com and navigate to the Course Reserves module within each course to submit both library reserve materials and bookstore additions at once. If you have not yet added your reserves, please contact me for assistance.

DocuServe
DocuServe is an information delivery service available to Caltech faculty, students, and staff. We participate in resource sharing partnerships and networks to provide you with alternative access to materials that we do not own or subscribe to. DocuServe includes InterLibrary Loan, document delivery, and more. Request materials related to your research, teaching, study, or employment at Caltech, such as books, journal articles, book chapters, theses, or media.

Finding U.S. Maps Online

Are you looking for geological or topographic maps online? This online class (October 5, 2023 at 2:00 pm) will show various websites where you can find free geologic, historical, and current topographic maps for the United States used in the geological sciences. You will learn how to navigate these sites to find exactly what you are looking for. Highly recommended if you are new to the geological sciences, but all are welcome.
